Knox's Rules

Click on each rule for a full explination

#1 - Don't get shot

As obvious as it sounds, it really is the key.

Don’t get too close in engagements unnecessarily, distant targets are harder to hit.

If you’re being shot at and not in a good position to return fire, then take cover.

Crouch down, or often better yet, lie down. Smaller targets are harder to hit.

Use cover where possible when crossing open areas.

Sprint only when necessary, it prevents your ability to return fire quickly.

#2 - Short, controlled bursts

Recoil is your enemy.

Burst fire allows for less recoil, greatly increasing accuracy.

By firing in burst, you will also preserve ammo, resulting in less reloads.

#3 - Know your distance

You'll need to know where to shoot.

Greater distance means you'll need a greater lead on a target and greater bullet drop.

#4 - Keep a full mag

To kill the enemy, you’ll want bullets, so keep an eye on your mag rounds.

If you’re running low, or if you run out of bullets, then reload.

If you’re not in combat, and can take good cover, then reload, even if you’ve only fired one round.

If you have support to protect you, and can take good cover, then reload.

#5 - If in doubt, fall back

Do not risk your life to hold ground that's not valuable.

Use smoke grenades to cover a retreat, or flash bangs / concussion grenades if available.

Suppression can also buy you some time for a retreat.

#6 - Keep them suppressed

It’s difficult to shoot and kill while being shot at.

Fire towards a target to prevent him from returning fire or advancing.
